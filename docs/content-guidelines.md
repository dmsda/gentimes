# GenTimes Content Guidelines for AI Overview Optimization

This document provides guidelines for creating content optimized for Google AI Overview citation.

---

## Core Principles

1. **Write for understanding, not keywords**
2. **Answer first, elaborate second**
3. **Be clear, factual, and concise**
4. **Structure content for extraction**

---

## Article Structure Template

### Required Sections

```
1. HEADLINE
   - Factual, descriptive
   - No clickbait
   - Include key entity/topic

2. INTRO (First 2-3 paragraphs)
   - What is this about?
   - Why does it matter?
   - Key takeaway

3. BODY
   - Descriptive H2/H3 headings
   - Short paragraphs (2-4 lines)
   - Bullet points for facts
   - Tables for comparisons

4. CONCLUSION
   - Summary of key points
   - What happens next
```

---

## Intro Paragraph Pattern

**DO:**
```
[Topic] is [clear definition]. [Why it matters to readers]. 
This article explains [scope] and what it means for [audience].
```

**DON'T:**
```
Have you ever wondered about...?
In today's fast-paced world...
The tech industry is buzzing about...
```

---

## Heading Guidelines

**Good (Descriptive):**
- "How Quantum Computing Works"
- "Key Benefits of AI in Healthcare"
- "Apple's 2025 Product Roadmap"

**Bad (Vague):**
- "What's Next"
- "The Good and Bad"
- "Our Thoughts"

---

## Content Types & Optimization

### News Article
- Lead with facts in first paragraph
- Include who, what, when, where
- Quote sources with attribution
- End with implications

### Explainer
- Start with clear definition
- Use progressive disclosure (simple → complex)
- Include FAQ section
- Use Definition components

### Analysis
- State thesis upfront
- Support with data/quotes
- Maintain fact/opinion separation
- Label opinion clearly

---

## Components to Use

| Component | When to Use |
|-----------|-------------|
| `KeyPoints` | Facts, summaries, quick takeaways |
| `Definition` | Technical terms, products, concepts |
| `ComparisonTable` | Feature comparisons, vs. articles |
| `QuoteBlock` | Expert statements, source attribution |
| `FAQSection` | Common questions about the topic |
| `Timeline` | Historical context, roadmaps |

---

## Entity Consistency

Use consistent naming throughout articles:

| Correct | Avoid Switching To |
|---------|-------------------|
| Apple | the Cupertino company, the iPhone maker |
| OpenAI | the AI startup, Sam Altman's company |
| GPT-4 | the large language model, the AI |

---

## Quality Checklist

Before publishing, verify:

- [ ] First 300 words could stand alone as a summary
- [ ] Headline is factual and descriptive
- [ ] H2/H3 headings summarize their sections
- [ ] Facts are clearly stated and verifiable
- [ ] Author and publish date are visible
- [ ] Internal links to related content exist
- [ ] No clickbait or filler paragraphs

---

## Example: Good vs. Bad Intro

### ❌ Bad
> Artificial intelligence is changing everything. From the way we work to the way 
> we play, AI is making waves across every industry. But what exactly is happening 
> with AI in 2025? Let's dive in and explore...

### ✅ Good
> OpenAI released GPT-5 on January 15, 2025, introducing multimodal reasoning 
> capabilities and a 1 million token context window. The model outperforms GPT-4 
> by 40% on reasoning benchmarks. This article explains GPT-5's key features, 
> pricing, and implications for developers and businesses.

---

## Remember

Write as if Google AI Overview is reading your article to answer a user's question. 
Your goal is to be the source that gets cited.
